If you have an underweight Body Mass Index (BMI), there are several things you can do to improve your BMI:

1.Consult with a healthcare professional: Before making any changes to your diet or exercise routine, it's important to consult with a healthcare professional such as a doctor or a dietitian. They can help you determine the best course of action for your specific needs.

2.Increase calorie intake: To increase your BMI, you'll need to consume more calories than you burn. This can be done by eating more nutrient-dense foods such as fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats.

3.Consume adequate protein: Protein is essential for building and repairing muscle tissue. Aim for at least 0.8 g/kg of body weight per day.

4.Include healthy fats in your diet: Healthy fats, such as olive oil, avocado, nuts, and seeds, are calorie-dense and can help you reach your calorie goals.

5.Use supplements: If you're having trouble getting enough nutrients from food alone, supplements such as protein powder, weight gainer, or a multivitamin may be helpful.

6.Exercise: Exercise can help you to build muscle and increase your overall calorie expenditure. Talk to your healthcare professional to help design a safe workout plan that's right for you.

7.Be patient: Gaining weight takes time, so be patient and don't get discouraged if you don't see results right away.

8.Stay consistent: Stick to a healthy diet and exercise routine and make adjustments as needed.

9.It's important to keep in mind that weight gain should be gradual and healthy, and it's not about gaining weight as fast as possible. Crash dieting or over-exercising can lead to negative consequences.

10.Also, it's important to note that a healthy weight gain is not only about increasing muscle mass but also about increasing bone density and overall health.
